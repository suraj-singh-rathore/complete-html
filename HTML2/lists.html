Challenges to Democracy: Indian democracy faces several challenges, including:

  1. Social and economic inequalities, such as poverty, illiteracy, and gender discrimination.
  2. Issues related to casteism and communalism, which are often exploited for political gains.
  3. Corruption and criminalization of politics.
  4. Threats from regionalism and separatism.
  5. Concerns regarding the functioning of democratic institutions and the weakening of civil society organizations.

Executive, Legislature, and Judiciary: The Union government comprises three main branches: the executive, the legislature, and the judiciary.

  • Executive: Headed by the Prime Minister and the Council of Ministers, responsible for running the Union government. The President of India is the head of state and commander-in-chief of the armed forces.
  • Legislature: A bicameral Parliament consisting of the Lok Sabha (lower house) and the Rajya Sabha (upper house). The Lok Sabha members are directly elected, while the Rajya Sabha members are indirectly elected by state legislatures.
  • Judiciary: Independent of the executive and legislature, with the Supreme Court at its apex, acting as the guardian and interpreter of the Constitution.
